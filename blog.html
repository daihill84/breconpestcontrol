The Silent Entry: Why Rats in Drains are Your Biggest Threat

Published: January 2, 2026

If you are hearing scratching in your loft or walls, the culprit might not be coming through the roof. In my 30 years as a field technician—from the streets of Neath to the rural farms of Brecon—I've found that over 70% of persistent rat issues start in the sewer.

How Rats Access Your Home

Rats are expert swimmers and can tread water for up to three days. They use the public sewer system as a highway. If there is a small crack in your pipework or a redundant junction under your floorboards, they will find it. Once they "break out" of the pipe, they follow the cavity walls all the way to your attic.

David's Professional Tip:

"Don't just keep putting poison down. It's a sticking-plaster fix. If the rats keep coming back, you need a CCTV Drain Survey to find the hole and a 316-grade steel valve to block it for good."
