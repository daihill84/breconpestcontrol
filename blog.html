Drainage Intelligence

CCTV Drain Surveys: The Secret Weapon Against Rats

If you are in **Ammanford** or **Ystradgynlais**, and you have recurring rat problems, the culprit is almost certainly the sewer. Rats are incredible swimmers; they can navigate "U-bends" and climb vertical clay pipes with ease.

How Rats Enter via Drains

In older Victorian properties, clay pipes often shift or crack. Rats find these "faults," dig through the earth, and enter the void beneath your home. From there, they follow the pipework up into the attic. Without a CCTV Drain Survey, you are just treating the symptoms, not the cause.

316-Grade Stainless Steel Rat Valves

Once we identify the entry point using our HD cameras, we install a one-way rat valve. This allows waste to flow out but prevents rats from entering. Unlike plastic "flap" valves which rats can chew through in minutes, our 316-grade steel valves are permanent and indestructible.

Traditional Skills

Traditional Mole Catching in the Towy Valley

Mole activity in **Llandeilo** and **Talgarth** peaks in the early Spring. For a farmer or a homeowner with a manicured lawn, a single mole can cause hundreds of pounds in damage to mower blades and silage quality.

Why Gassing Doesn't Work

Many pest controllers offer "gas" treatments. In the heavy clay soil of the Beacons, gas often fails to penetrate the deep tunnels, or it leaks out of the many "surface runs." It is expensive and often ineffective.

The Art of the Trapper

As a traditional mole catcher, I don't use chemicals. I map the mole's "main runs" and set specialist traps that provide an instant, humane kill. This is the only method I recommend for **Red Tractor Compliant** farms, as it leaves no toxic residue in the soil.
