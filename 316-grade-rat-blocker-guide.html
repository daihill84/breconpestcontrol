If you have discovered rats entering your property via the drainage system, a standard plastic flap will not suffice. In the acidic, high-moisture environment of a sewer, 316-grade stainless steel is the only material that guarantees a permanent, poison-free solution.

Rats can easily gnaw through plastic, lead, and even low-grade 304 steel. As a specialist in Mid-Wales infrastructure, I install marine-grade 316 blockers designed to withstand the harshest subterranean conditions.

Why 316-Grade Steel is Essential

Most commercial blockers use 304-grade steel. While common, it often fails in sewer lines due to corrosion. We exclusively use 316 Marine Grade for three critical reasons:

  • Molybdenum Enhanced: Specifically resistant to corrosive hydrogen sulfide gases.
  • 7,000 PSI Protection: Proven to withstand the extreme gnawing force of rodents.
  • Non-Return Mechanism: Prevents rats from moving upstream while allowing free waste flow.

The Installation Process (LD3, SA18, SA9)

Serving Brecon, Llandovery, Sennybridge, and Ystradgynlais, our process ensures the integrity of your drainage system:

  1. HD CCTV Survey: We locate entry points and check for structural pipe defects.
  2. Debris Clearing: Chambers are cleaned to ensure the blocker seats perfectly.
  3. Precision Fitting: Tension-fit installation to the pipe outlet—no drilling required.
  4. Verification: High-volume flow tests to ensure zero drainage interference.
