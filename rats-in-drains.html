The Technical Solution to Rats in Drains

If you're dealing with persistent rat problems in your home or business, a CCTV drain survey for rats could be the key to identifying and resolving the issue. Around 90% of internal rat infestations originate from drainage defects, such as blind junctions, redundant pipes, or collapsed sections. These flaws create direct entry points for rats into buildings, often via wall cavities. Rats are excellent swimmers and can travel upstream through lateral drains, exploiting vulnerabilities to access properties in areas like SA9, SA18, and LD3.

How CCTV Drain Surveys Detect Rats

A CCTV drain survey for rats involves inserting a specialized push-rod camera into your drainage system to visually inspect pipes, joints, and potential entry points. This high-definition camera can navigate through the network, identifying cracks, breaks, or disused connections that allow rats to enter. For instance, rats can squeeze through holes as small as 13mm, so even minor defects like collapsed sections or blind junctions become gateways into your property.

During the survey, we look for signs of rodent activity, such as droppings, nesting areas in low-flow sections, or soil buildup in manholes. Real-time footage helps us locate nesting spots where rats build burrows in blockages or quieter pipe areas. This precise detection is crucial for effective rat control, as it guides targeted repairs and prevents recurring infestations. Our pan-and-tilt cameras allow us to examine side connections and upward pipes, ensuring no entry point is missed.

Unlike traditional methods, a CCTV drain survey for rats is non-destructive, saving time and money while providing a comprehensive view of your drainage system's condition. If you're in Brecon or surrounding areas and suspect rats in drains, this service is essential for lasting solutions.

FAQs About Rats in Drains and CCTV Surveys

Why do rats live in drains?

Rats thrive in sewer systems due to access to food scraps, water, and shelter. They can enter properties through defects in private drains, making a CCTV drain survey for rats essential to identify entry points.

How can I tell if there are rats in my drains?

Signs include unusual noises in walls or under floors, droppings near drains, foul odors, or slow drainage. If you suspect an issue, book a CCTV drain survey for rats to confirm and locate the problem.

Who is responsible for rats in drains?

If rats are in your private drainage system, it's your responsibility as the property owner. For public sewers, contact your local water company. A professional CCTV survey can determine the source.

Are stainless steel rat blockers effective?

Yes, they prevent rats from entering while allowing waste to pass. Made from durable 316-grade steel, they resist corrosion and gnawing, offering long-term protection without chemicals.

What happens during a CCTV drain survey for rats?

A high-definition camera is inserted into the pipes to inspect for defects, entry points, and signs of nesting. It's non-invasive and provides real-time insights for targeted rat control.

Can rats get into my home through drains?

Yes, via cracks or broken pipes. Rats can swim upstream, so faulty drains are a common entry route. Installing rat blockers after a survey prevents this.
